How to Change Your Own Oil in 5 Easy Steps

Changing your car's oil is one of the most fundamental and important maintenance tasks you can perform. It helps keep your engine running smoothly and extends its life. While it might seem daunting, it's a straightforward process you can do right in your driveway.

1. Gather Your Tools & Materials

Before you start, make sure you have everything you need. This includes the correct type and amount of new engine oil, a new oil filter, a wrench to remove the drain plug, an oil filter wrench, a drain pan, and some rags.

lightbulb

Pro Tip

Warm up your engine for 2-3 minutes before draining the oil. This helps the oil flow out more easily and quickly.

3. Drain the Old Oil

Once your car is safely elevated, slide underneath and locate the oil drain plug. Place your drain pan beneath it, then use your wrench to loosen the plug. Unscrew it the rest of the way by hand and let the old oil drain completely.

warning

Caution

Used engine oil is a hazardous material. Dispose of it properly at an auto parts store or recycling center.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use synthetic oil? expand_more

Yes, you can use synthetic oil if your vehicle's manufacturer recommends it. Always check your owner's manual for the correct oil type and viscosity grade.

How tight should the oil filter be? expand_more

Typically, you should tighten the oil filter by hand until it's snug, then give it an additional quarter-turn with an oil filter wrench. Do not over-tighten.
