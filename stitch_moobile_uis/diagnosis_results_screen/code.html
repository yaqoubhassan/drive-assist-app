What's Happening

info

The alternator is a critical component of your vehicle's charging system. It recharges the battery while the engine is running and powers the electrical system. A malfunction means your battery isn't being charged, which will eventually lead to a dead battery and vehicle shutdown.

What You Can Try expand_more
warning

Proceed with caution. These steps involve working near the engine and electrical components. If you are unsure, please consult a professional.

  1. Check Battery Terminals: Ensure the connections on your battery are tight and free of corrosion.
  2. Inspect the Serpentine Belt: Check the belt that drives the alternator for any signs of cracking, fraying, or looseness.
  3. Listen for Noises: A failing alternator can sometimes make a grinding or whining noise.

Safety Warnings

  • electric_bolt Risk of electric shock. Disconnect the battery before inspecting electrical components.
  • do_not_disturb_on Do not operate the vehicle. Driving may cause further damage and sudden loss of power.
